# Open Research Questions

## Grounding

- How can we ensure every visual claim maps to evidence in the image/video/document?
- Can patch-level alignment become interpretable and reliable?
- How should models expose uncertainty when visual evidence is weak?

## Video understanding

- How many frames are enough?
- When is frame sampling insufficient?
- How should long video memory work?
- How do we evaluate temporal causality rather than static recognition?

## Multimodal reasoning

- Do VLMs reason visually or translate perception into language and reason textually?
- How do chain-of-thought and hidden reasoning interact with visual grounding?
- Can visual reasoning be verified step-by-step?

## Training data

- How much synthetic instruction data is too much?
- How do we detect visually ungrounded teacher-generated answers?
- How do we weight OCR/chart/spatial/video data?

## Alignment

- How should preference labels incorporate visual evidence?
- How do we avoid degrading text-only abilities during visual tuning?
- What does harmlessness mean for images, documents, screenshots, medical imagery, or surveillance footage?

## Agents

- What is the right action space for GUI/browser agents?
- How do we verify that a visual action succeeded?
- How do we prevent models from clicking destructive UI elements?
